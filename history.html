History

The line runs unbroken.

Two Pretoria practices, each with its own century behind it, joined forces in 2009. This is how the firm you brief today was assembled.

1898

Schalk Willem Pienaar opens practice

The founding line of Solomon & Nicolson begins in Pretoria, four years before the end of the Anglo-Boer War.

1902

Pienaar and Marais

Rupert Ford Marais joins in partnership. The firm later becomes Marais Price and Clark under Norman Price and Charles William Clark.

1927

Clark Solomon & Nicolson

Malcolm Graham Nicolson — former Secretary of the Transvaal Law Society and Town Clerk of Pretoria — joins the firm.

1930

Friedland Hart & Partners formed

The practices of Sidney “Sonny” Charles Hart and David “Ivy” Israel Friedland amalgamate. Sonny Hart later serves in the South African Forces and is awarded the Order of the British Empire.

1991

Solomon Nicolson Rein & Verster

Solomon & Nicolson merges with Rein & Verster; from 2006 the practice is known simply as Solomon Nicolson.

1998

A centenary

Solomon Nicolson marks one hundred years of continuous practice in Pretoria.

2009

Friedland Hart Solomon & Nicolson

The two longstanding practices join forces under a single name and style — the firm as it stands today.

Today

Painter at the helm

Gerhard Painter, with the firm since 1982, is Managing Partner. Millie Shantall-Lurie has practised here since 1995.
